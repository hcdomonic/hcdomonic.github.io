Abstract: There are three major applications (portable, stationary,
transportation) of hydrogen energy which optimal development environment
are different now, a country should not develop only one application of
hydrogen energy in all regions for the sustainability problem of the
existing energy system, so this paper explores the optimal multiple
selections process for the different fostering applications of hydrogen
energy by different regions in a country by the Analytical Hierarchy
Process (AHP) and a fuzzy Technique the Order of Preference by Similarity
to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS) method. Based on literature review and the in-
depth interviews with officers, experts, the managers of hydrogen energy-
related enterprises, and consumers, the selection criteria are the
acceptance on the applications of hydrogen energy (market entry barriers
and customer's acceptance), and the integrity of hydrogen energy
industrial chain on their three applications (its production stage, its
distribution stage, its marketing stage). This paper used a case study to
arrange three applications of hydrogen energy in the industrial, cultural
and restricted residence regions, then to prove the executability of the
above process. The following findings are obtained: 1. The market
structure on the portable, stationary and transportation application of
hydrogen energy should be a pure competitive, oligopoly, and monopolistic
competition. 2. The customer's acceptance for its different applications
are different for there are different neighborhood concerns about the
quality of life (NIMBY) and consumer issues. 3. The cost and benefit
analysis of different applications depends on some different factors,
including government policy, technological improvements and social
preferences. 4. For all applications need to promote, the multiple
selections on the different fostering applications of hydrogen energy in
different regions of a country depend on the comparative advantages of
the different applications.

1.Introduction
Hydrogen energy (HE) has been the solution of many countries, enterprises and
scholars to solve the sustainability problem of the existing energy system, as the
bigger gap between the decreasing supply and increasing demand of energy in the
same energy price, air pollution, energy security, greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions,
and climate change. Moreover, the recent problem is the more risks for the existing
power grids from the growing proportion of renewable energies. The subject of HE is
hydrogen for it is an ideal energy carrier with less polluting, the major device to use
HE is the fuel cell (FC), and electrolysis and FCs could offer HE a bi-directional link
among the energy carriers. The governments of China, USA, Japan, Canada, and EU
have coordinated the HE industry in their national economies for many years, and
tried to clamp down the sales of internal combustion engine (ICE) car and promoted
HE for environmental protection and human health. Yang et al. (2013) portrayed
China used national strategies, research programs, policies, rules, regulations, subsidies,
technology and emissions standards to promote HE. USA Department of Energy (2011)
committed to loans or grants factories for HE and supported the refueling points
demonstration projects and tax credits of HE by the Recovery Act. The Empowering
Tunisian Renewable Energy Research Activities
(ETRERA) is an international
cooperation project between Tunisia and European countries to enhance Tunisian
knowledge on HE technologies and applications. Bayerische Motoren Werke AG
(BMW) in Germany has obtained the loans of European Investment Bank to develop
HE cars. Choi et al. (2011) thought the knowledge network of HE should be driven by
the Korea government to facilitate the sharing of risks and the solidifying of HE
knowledge. (Riahi and Roehrl, 2000; Casaramona et al., 2015; Grigolon et al., 2015;
Nhamo, 2014; Egbue and Long, 2012)
There is a gap between the supply and demand of energy of the world could be
proved by its growth rates of economic and energy consumption. Its growth rate of
CO2 emissions could prove the GHG emissions problem of the existing energy
system. However, HE is just suitable to small scale operations now which could be
proved by the power mix of G20; the reasons should be the difficulty to produce,
distribute and market the applications of HE. In 2017 of G20, which accounts for 80%
of global consumption, its economic growth rate at purchasing power parity is 3.7%;
its annual increment and growth rate of energy consumption are 11.1 gigatons of oil
equivalent (GtoE) and 2.1%. Its annual increment and growth rate of
emissions,
which is a major form of energy combustion, are 27 gigatons and 2.0%. Crude oil
(32.0%), coal (27.0%), natural gas (22.0%) are its main energies consumed; total
renewables which are hydro, wind, solar, geothermal, and biomass rank second in its
power mix. (Enerdata, 2018a, 2018b)
Due to the lack of competitiveness of HE, government should use policies to
promote the portable, stationary and transportation applications of HE, and from the
existing experiences of many countries and works of literature, the government should
not ignore anyone application of HE for many issues as science, technology, politics,
business, economics, society. The applications of HE could be classified as portable
(as gensets, back-up power and specialist vehicles), stationary (micro and industrial
combined heat and power (CHP), large power), and transportation (cars, light-duty
vehicles
(LDVs), buses, auxiliary power units
(road, sea, rail)). For Japan, the
researching and developing applications of HE are portable FC, Hydrogen power
generation/FC for business and industry, FC bus, FC railroad vehicle, and HE jet
aircraft. As Mintzer et al. (2003) found HE should have the potential to yield the
benefits of economic, environmental, and energy security; but its potential would be
different for different government policy, a government committed to develop the
production of HE and the usage on transportation and stationary applications of HE
for energy security, it would accelerate the popularity of HE. If the government used
policy to force faster reductions in oil demand for the GHG emissions problem, it
would result in more usage of hybrid gasoline-electric and diesel-electric vehicles. In
the other side, if the government does not take any action about HE, there is less
chance for people to use HE. (Önü and Soner, 2008; Ministry of Economy, Trade, and
Industry in Japan, 2018a)
For the suitable development region of HE’s different application is different, the
liberalization of the energy markets and the integration of energy and transport
networks, government promotion policies of He should be a trans-regional issue.
From the review of literature and the interviews with experts, the relevant criteria for
a country to select the optimal developed application of HE in a specific region should
be the demand and supply side of HE industry; its criteria of demand side are the
acceptant cost-benefit comparisons on the HE's applications (market entry barriers
and customer's acceptance); its criteria of supply side are the establishment
cost-benefit comparisons for the integrity of HE industrial chain on their three
applications (their production stage, their distribution stage, their marketing stage).
Many works of literature thought the usage of HE depends on GDP, the consumption
and investment of energy, consumption, economic growth, government policy,
technological improvements, and social preferences. For Japan, the government
developed a model for realizing a HE society which He is produced from renewable
energy and demonstrated for transporting and storing, as a large scale demonstration
base of hydrogen production from renewable energy in Namie Town. (Enerdata,
2018a, 2018b; Ministry of Economy, Trade, and Industry in Japan, 2018a; Salahuddin
et al., 2018; Sharaf, 2016; Dogan, 2014; Egbue and Long, 2012; Eggoh et al., 2011;
Önü and Soner, 2008; Takeshita et al., 2006)
The decision-making process of the government to pick up the best application of
HE should be complex and uncertain, and three applications of HE should be
promoted in the same time, so its best application should be adjusted to be its
comparatively better application. This paper would consider the Multiple Criteria
Decision Making
(MCDM) fuzzy methodology which included the concept of
Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) and Fuzzy Technique for Order of Preference by
Similarity to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS) method, then studied the above results by the
analysis of their comparative advantages to get three applications of HE with their
comparatively better regions of a country. For the targets of this paper are fairly
apparent that this research requires a flexible and open-ended approach to capture the
respondents’ opinions thought processes, and the use of the AHP and a fuzzy TOPSIS
method, rather than a survey would better serve the objectives of this study.

2. The Method to Select Three Applications of HE with Their Comparatively
Better Regions
HE could be the substitute with less GHG emissions for fossil fuels, and the energy
carrier of renewable energies, but there are the supply and demand problems of
energy markets for the government to promote HE energy, and these problems would
be different with different applications of HE and different regions. A wealth of
literature has addressed and proven the necessity to develop HE. There is no literature
has ever discussed the relationships among the three applications of HE and the
regions which would be used to develop HE. This article shows its theoretical
contribution and uniqueness by adopting an MCDM fuzzy methodology and the
analysis of comparative advantage to get the solution of the multiple selections
problem about three applications of HE with their comparatively better regions of a
country. Lima and Veziroglu (2001) found the introduction of HE would increase the
energy consumption, gross national product per capita, and the quality of life in
Brazil.

4. Conclusion
There are more and more countries to explore the future possibilities on HE's
mobile, stationary and transport applications with the purposes of comprehensive
energy conservation, promotion the competitiveness of HE industry maximized
introduction of renewable energy, and the reduction of the public burden about
international environmental negotiations. However, the costs and benefits of three
HE's applications are different in different regions, and a country should not ignore
anyone application of HE for its every application has its specific importance. Thus,
this paper set the optimal selection process for three HE's applications in different
regions of a country which included AHP, fuzzy TOPSIS, and the analysis of
comparative advantages. From the results of the literature review and the in-depth
interviews with consumers and experts in the industry, government and school, the
criteria of the above process are the net benefits of market entry, customer's
acceptance, the stages of HE industrial chain (production, distribution, and marketing).
(Ministry of Economy, Trade, and Industry in Japan, 2018b)
By a case study which arranges three HE's applications in the industrial, cultural
and restricted residence regions, and the executability of the above process is proved.
The other findings are 1. The net benefit of market entry by three applications of HE
is different from its market structure, and its sequence from big to small might be
portable, transport, and stationary applications of HE. 2. The net benefit of customer’s
acceptance should be different from the socio-economic conditions of the region, and
its sequence from big to small might be cultural, industrial, and restricted residence
region. 3. The net benefit of the production stage of the HE industrial chain should be
different from the HE industrial conditions of the region, and it might be largest in the
industrial region. 4. The net benefit on the distribution stage of the HE industrial chain
should be different from its refueling frequency, and it of HE's transport application
might be the least. 5. The net benefit on the marketing stage of the HE industrial chain
should be different from its market scale, and of HE's transport application might be
the most. 3. 6. The cost and benefit analysis of different HE’s applications depends on
some different factors, as government policy, technological improvements and social
preferences.
4. For all applications of HE need to promote, the analysis of
comparative advantages could help the government select the different fostering
applications of hydrogen energy in different regions of a country.
The suggestions for the government: 1. The promotion policies should consider
the demand and supply of HE market, as the cost-benefit analysis of market entry,
customer's acceptance, and the production, distribution, and marketing stages of HE
industrial chain. 2. The targets and contents of HE’s promotion policies should be
different from the characters of different regions.
The future study could focus the contradiction between the distribution stage and
marketing stage of HE industry, because of it is hard for consumers to use the
applications of HE with less refueling stations, but the sufficient infrastructure of HE
could be provided only for the adequate consumers’ demand. Especially for the
transport application of HE, the effects of lock-in and path dependence by transport
application of gasoline are significant for the existing infrastructure. As Huang et al.
(2010) used a stochastic dynamic programming model for the building process on the
production and distribution stages of HE, the decision variables are their sequences,
locations, and costs. In the other side, the other technique could be used to enhance
the selection model could be suitable to real conditions of the evaluating case, as the
analytic network process, cost point methods, data envelopment analysis, linear
programming. (Cecere et al. 2014)
